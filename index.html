Research

Localization-Aware Test-Time Defense for Sparse-Contamination RAG

Undergraduate Thesis, 2025–2026

  • Studying test-time defenses against indirect prompt injection attacks in multi-source retrieval-augmented generation systems.
  • Designing a localization-aware defense pipeline that identifies high-risk retrieved segments before applying stronger intervention.
  • Evaluating attack success rate, clean-task utility, and defense-side token cost across multiple open-source language models and QA datasets.
  • Comparing localized defenses with global baselines such as perplexity-based filtering and randomized smoothing.
